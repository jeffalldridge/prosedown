# The Trailhead

The parking lot was empty at five in the morning. Elena pulled the truck alongside the trail register and killed the engine. Through the windshield, the Sawtooths cut black against a sky just beginning to remember blue.

She checked her pack one more time. Water, map, first aid, rain shell. Everything she needed. Nothing she didn't.

The trail started gentle — packed dirt through lodgepole pines, the creek running easy beside her. She'd be above treeline by noon.
