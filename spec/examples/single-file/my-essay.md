# On the Nature of Simple Things

The simplest things are often the most profound. This essay explores why complexity is the enemy of understanding, and how returning to fundamentals reveals truth that sophistication obscures.

## The Case for Simplicity

When we strip away the unnecessary, what remains is essential. This is not minimalism for its own sake — it is clarity earned through discipline.

> "Perfection is achieved, not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." — Antoine de Saint-Exupery

## What We Lose When We Add

Every addition to a system introduces not just the thing itself, but the interactions between that thing and everything else. Complexity grows quadratically while perceived value grows linearly.

## Returning to First Principles

The path back to simplicity requires courage. It means admitting that much of what we built was unnecessary. It means choosing to do less, better.
